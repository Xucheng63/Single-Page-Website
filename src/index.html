The Super Mario Brothers

Super Mario Brothers is a side-scrolling action game for Family Computer developed by Nintendo's Intelligence Development Division. One day, the great turtles with powerful magic invaded the peaceful kingdom where the mushrooms lived. The honest mushroom family has been transformed into rocks, bricks or pen vegetables and other shapes by the cool overlord, and the mushroom kingdom is about to perish. Can unlock the magic of the mushrooms, let them wake up, only the princess of the mushroom kingdom - Princess Peach blossom. However, she was captured by the great evil Lord Cool Overlord. In order to bring down the turtle family, rescue the peach blossom princess, and bring back peace to the mushroom kingdom, Mario decided to stand up and challenge the cool overlord.

The Legend of Zelda

The Legend of Zelda is a series of games produced and published by Nintendo since 1986. The game is set in a fictional fantasy world, and for most of the game players will control Link and embark on adventures across the land of Helalu in order to rescue Princess Zelda from enemies such as Ganondorf.

Pokemon

Pokemon is a series of games developed by Game Freak and Creatures Inc. and published by Nintendo as a major part of Pokemon media. The original Game Boy role-playing game was released in Japan in 1996. The Pokemon series has been used throughout Nintendo's console generations. Games in the series are usually released in pairs, with remastered versions often appearing several years after the release of the original game. The main series consists of role-playing games, while the side series includes other genres, such as action role-playing games, puzzle games, and Tamagotchi. February 27 is "Pokemon Day".

Influence

As a popular series, the Pokemon series has undoubtedly left its mark in popular culture. The Pokemon featured in the series have also become pop culture ICONS: two different Pikachu balloons in the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, All Nippon Airways' Pokemon jet, thousands of spin-off merchandise, the Pokemon parks in Nagoya and Taipei, and more.

As of March 31, 2013, the Pokemon series has sold 245 million copies, making it one of the best-selling games in gaming history. Guinness World Records awarded Pokemon games eight records in the 2008 Guinness World Records Gamer Edition, including "the most successful RPG series of all time" and "the game series with the most spin-off movies."

Japan Memorial Day Association recognized February 27 as "Pokemon Day".
